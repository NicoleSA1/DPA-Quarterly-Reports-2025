Fourth Quarter 2025 Report: October - December 2025 2025 ANNUAL REPORT

2025 Annual Executive Summary

In 2025, the San Francisco Department of Police Accountability received 899 complaints — a 22% increase over 2024 — while simultaneously reducing average case processing time from 116 days to 93 days, well below the 180-day department target, and meeting the statutory Government Code 3304 one-year deadline 100% of the time. Of 2,012 allegations investigated, the DPA sustained findings in 35 cases, with body-worn camera compliance violations accounting for the majority of sustained allegations. These results reflect both growing community engagement with the accountability process and meaningful operational improvements by the DPA.

SFSO Oversight - Q4 2025

SFSO SFSO Oversight

The DPA has investigated certain types of serious complaints against deputies of the San Francisco Sheriff's Office (SFSO) since 2018, ensuring independent oversight, reinforcing transparency, and promoting accountability across jail operations, courthouse security, and broader SFSO activities. Through formal agreements and evolving mandates, including oversight of in-custody deaths and the use of military equipment, the DPA has expanded its role to support investigations, critical incidents, and community engagement.

SFSO DPA Authority Under Letter of Agreement

Under a Letter of Agreement (LOA) with the San Francisco SFSO's Office, DPA investigates serious misconduct cases involving SFSO's deputies. DPA's oversight authority includes independent investigations of:

  • In-custody deaths
  • Complaints of:
    • Use of force - injury or death
    • Use of a weapon or control device
    • Sexual misconduct
    • Reckless disregard for health or safety
    • Pattern or practice of harassment, bias, or retaliation by SFSO's Deputies
    • Additional misconduct at SFSO discretion

DPA submits investigative findings to the SFSO and provides quarterly reports to the SFSO's Oversight Board on complaint statistics and investigation status.

Investigations Division

The Investigations Division continued to demonstrate excellence in conducting thorough, impartial investigations throughout 2025. The team successfully maintained the 100% compliance rate with Government Code 3304 one-year deadline requirements while significantly improving case processing efficiency. Investigators handled increasingly complex cases involving emerging technologies, including body-worn camera analysis and digital evidence review, while maintaining the highest standards of investigative thoroughness.

Legal Team

The Legal Team provided critical support to the DPA's mission throughout 2025, ensuring all investigations and findings met legal standards and withstood scrutiny. The team successfully defended DPA findings in appeals and administrative proceedings, maintaining a strong track record of upholding sustained findings. Public Records Division

The Public Records Division managed a substantial increase in California Public Records Act (CPRA) requests during 2025, reflecting heightened public interest in police accountability data. The division successfully processed requests within legal timeframes while protecting confidential information and complainant privacy. Implementation of improved tracking systems enhanced response efficiency and transparency.

Beyond responding to records requests, the division contributed to the DPA's transparency initiatives by proactively publishing aggregate data, quarterly reports, and policy documents on the department website. Audit Division

The Audit Division continued conducting independent, objective audits of the San Francisco Police Department (SFPD). These audits are intended to promote effectiveness, efficiency, and accountability within SFPD.

In May, the DPA received its third consecutive Knighton Award from the Association of Local Government Auditors (ALGA) for its audit on the completeness and accuracy of data SFPD is required to report to the California Department of Justice under the Racial and Identity Profiling Act of 2015. ALGA judges praised the report for its thorough documentation, strong use of visuals, and well-structured presentation.

In December, DPA issued a Charter-mandated audit of SFPD’s use of force. This audit evaluated the effectiveness of SFPD’s processes for monitoring use of force incidents, including supervisors’ evaluations for policy compliance and how the department uses data to inform training. DPA made 14 recommendations to strengthen SFPD’s use-of-force oversight.

Policy Division

The Policy Division played a central role in advancing police accountability reforms through comprehensive policy analysis and development. The division reviewed SFPD policies for alignment with best practices, constitutional standards, and community expectations, providing recommendations to the Police Commission on policy updates and new regulations. Throughout 2025, the division analyzed emerging issues in law enforcement, including technology use, de-escalation techniques, and bias prevention.

Conclusion

The Department of Police Accountability made significant strides in 2025 toward improving efficiency, transparency, and accountability in police oversight. Through dedicated work across all divisions—Investigations, Legal, Public Records, Audit, Policy, and the Intern Program—the DPA strengthened San Francisco’s police accountability framework. While challenges remain, particularly in areas of body-worn camera compliance and mediation success rates, the department's commitment to continuous improvement positions it well for continued progress in 2026.

The DPA remains dedicated to its mission of providing independent, thorough, and impartial investigations of complaints against San Francisco law enforcement, while working to strengthen community trust and promote accountability.
